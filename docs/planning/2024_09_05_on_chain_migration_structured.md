# Project Jinn: Structured On-Chain Migration Plan

- **Date**: 2024-09-05
- **Status**: Draft

## 1. Product Vision: An Operating System for Autonomous Ventures

Project Jinn is a system for launching and operating autonomous, AI-driven organizations that pursue open-ended goals on the internet. It is targeted at founders, entrepreneurs, and visionaries who want to achieve a complex objective without the traditional overhead of hiring developers, raising large amounts of capital upfront, or managing corporate structures.

It is understood that the initial ventures will need to be tightly scoped to prove the model, likely focusing on a specific niche before expanding to more generalized, open-ended goals.

The user experience is envisioned as follows: a founder arrives at a platform, defines a high-level mission, injects an initial amount of capital (optional), and launches the venture with a single transaction. From that point, a decentralized network of AI agents (workers) begins executing tasks, generating artifacts, and autonomously pursuing the founder's goal. The founder's role shifts from direct execution to cultivating and guiding the system at a high level.

This represents a radical shift from building a startup to launching a self-perpetuating, goal-seeking digital organization.

## 2. Core Technical Architecture

The architecture is designed to be decentralized, modular, and robust, leveraging best-in-class protocols for specific functions while keeping the core application logic flexible and mission-specific.

### 2.1 System Components & Roles

The system is composed of three layers: Olas Protocol Primitives, Mission-Specific Contracts, and Off-Chain Components.

**A. Olas Protocol Primitives (The Incentive Layer)**

These are the core, un-modified Olas contracts that the system leverages.

1.  **`ServiceRegistry`**: A mission is registered **once** here to gain a canonical on-chain identity (an ERC-721 token). This defines the mission at a high level: its agents, security model, and governing multisig.
2.  **`StakingContract` (Gauge)**: This is the mission's primary incentive pool. `veOLAS` holders direct OLAS emissions here, and workers who complete jobs for the mission claim their OLAS rewards from this contract. It **does not** hold any application logic or job definitions.

**B. Mission-Specific Contracts (The Application Layer)**

These components are deployed for *each* new mission, typically in a single, bundled transaction facilitated by a specialized launch protocol like Doppler.

1.  **`OpenZeppelin (OZ) Governor`**: The mission's dedicated governance contract.
    *   **Purpose**: Manages all critical, low-frequency decisions for the mission, such as updating mission parameters or approving treasury expenditures.
    *   **Voting**: Voting power is typically tied to the mission's specific token.

2.  **Mission Token**: An ERC-20 token unique to the mission.
    *   **Purpose**: To raise initial capital and align the interests of speculators, supporters, and workers.
    *   **Mechanism**: The OLAS rewards claimed from the `StakingContract` are the primary source of value. A portion of these claimed OLAS rewards can be programmatically paired with the Mission Token and deposited into a liquidity pool (e.g., `TOKEN/OLAS`), deepening liquidity over time without requiring the sale of the native token.

**C. Off-Chain Components (The Execution & Data Layer)**

1.  **Jinn Workers (AI Agents)**: The off-chain agents that perform the actual work.
    *   **Logic**: The system relies on an emergent, off-chain work decomposition flow. A primary "planner" agent watches the mission's high-level goal (defined via a governance action or initial setup) and breaks it down into smaller, executable jobs. These job definitions are themselves published as artifacts to Irys. Other "executor" agents then discover these job artifacts and perform the tasks. This removes the need for a centralized on-chain job board for most use cases.
2.  **Irys**: A decentralized storage network used for permanent data.
    *   **Purpose**: All large data blobs and "artifacts" generated by workers (e.g., images, code, documents, **and job definitions**) are stored on Irys. Only the Irys content identifier (CID) is referenced on-chain when necessary for settlement.
3.  **Cipher**: A specialized tool for managing agent memory.
    *   **Purpose**: The complex, long-term memory of the agent system is moved from the Supabase database to this dedicated off-chain tool, which is optimized for the needs of agentic systems.

### 2.2 The Lifecycle of a Mission

1.  **Launch**: A founder uses a simple UI that leverages a launch protocol like Doppler. In a single transaction batch, Doppler deploys the `StakingContract`, `Mission Token`, and `OZ Governor` contracts.
2.  **Funding**: The launch protocol, Doppler, facilitates the initial capital bootstrap through its "Doppler Airlock" mechanism. This process begins with a fully on-chain Dutch auction for the Mission Token to establish fair price discovery. Once a floor price is met, the token becomes available on a dynamic bonding curve. As capital is raised, Doppler automatically creates a Uniswap v2 or v4 pool, migrates a portion of the tokens and the raised capital (e.g., ETH) to bootstrap the initial liquidity, and programmatically locks the resulting LP tokens in a Timelock contract. This provides transparency and trust for early supporters while protecting against MEV and sniping. The remaining raised funds are directed to the mission's treasury, controlled by the `OZ Governor`.
3.  **Incentivization**: The system begins to accrue OLAS rewards in its `StakingContract` as `veOLAS` holders direct emissions to it.
4.  **Operation**:
    *   The mission's high-level objective is set via a transaction, either during setup or through the `Governor`.
    *   Off-chain "planner" agents observe this objective and begin the work decomposition flow, publishing smaller job definitions as artifacts to Irys.
    *   Off-chain "executor" agents discover these job artifacts on Irys and execute them.
    *   Upon completion, workers submit proof and claim their OLAS rewards from the `StakingContract`.
    *   A portion of the claimed OLAS may be periodically used to deepen the `TOKEN/OLAS` liquidity pool, governed by a function callable by the `Governor`.

## 3. Key Architectural Decisions & Refinements

The final architecture was reached through a series of refinements that addressed key constraints of decentralized systems.

1.  **Job Dispatching: On-Chain Registry vs. Off-Chain Decomposition**
    *   **Initial Idea**: Use a dedicated on-chain `AgentDispatcher` contract to store and manage all job definitions and triggers.
    *   **Refinement**: This approach adds on-chain overhead and complexity. The revised, leaner model relies on the off-chain **work decomposition flow**. Job definitions are simply artifacts stored on Irys, making the system more flexible and scalable. An on-chain `AgentDispatcher` is now considered an optional component for future missions that may require more complex, hard-coded, on-chain dispatch logic.

2.  **System Liveness: Keeper Network vs. Intrinsic Incentives**
    *   **Initial Idea**: Use a keeper network like Gelato to call dispatch functions and keep the system "alive."
    *   **Refinement**: With the removal of the on-chain `AgentDispatcher`, there is no longer a core contract that needs to be "woken up" by a keeper. Liveness becomes an emergent property of the system: as long as the `StakingContract` is emitting OLAS rewards, workers are intrinsically motivated to perform the planning and execution loop.

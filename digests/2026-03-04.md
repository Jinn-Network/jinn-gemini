## Architecture decisions

- **TEMPLATE_OUTPUT tagging**: Shifted from output spec approach (wasn't working) to prompt-based artifact tagging. Simpler, works now. The synthesis child job — not the root job — is what produces the final content.
- **Agent Attention Market proposal**: Early-stage exploration of replacing OLAS/Mech marketplace with a composition of ERC-8004 (identity + validation registries), A2A (discovery), AP2 (bounty payments), and x402 (settlement). The "node" would shrink from a full OLAS service to a lightweight cron job + wallet. Spec: `docs/spec/document-bounty.md`.
- **ADW implementation paused**: Document registry function may be fully covered by ERC-8004's Identity Registry (already type-agnostic). ADW could become an ERC-8004 profile rather than a standalone spec.
- **Private IPFS identified as migration-proof**: Regardless of OLAS vs. open standards direction, private venture data storage remains critical infrastructure.

## Open questions

- Can AP2 handle publisher-pays-reader payment direction, or do we need a thin escrow contract?
- What level of verification is needed for proof-of-read? Trust-based (node attestation), TEE, or output-based?
- Does the simplicity of the open standards approach eliminate the need for a token entirely, or is one still needed for bootstrapping?
- Migration vs. rebuild: can we milk OLAS staking rewards during a gradual transition?
- What's the moat once we ship on open standards? ERC-8004 reputation accumulation? Private IPFS? Network effects?
